01 : Medical Simulation

VR-based Hi-fidelity digital rectal examination setup with haptic feedback

The incidence rate of prostate cancer in India as of September 2019 data by Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) is 9-10/10,000. The conventional method for examining the growth of tumour in the prostate gland is an intuitive procedure that involves palpation of the prostate gland by inserting a finger into the rectum through the anus, known as the Digital Rectal Examination. This method is simple to use, cheap and painless, but highly subjective as it is dependent on the experience of the physician. Due to the lack of subjects, physicians are not trained sufficiently to diagnose the clinical condition or determine the growth stage of tumour using this procedure. The aim of this project is to design a setup modelled using the biomechanical properties of the rectum and the prostate along with the incorporation of the sensors that will provide visual as well as haptic feedback to the user. This project involves the display of the real-time movements of the user’s hand like the rotation, translation and finger flexion in a virtual reality setup that includes a rectal model in a clinical environment which reacts corresponding to the user’s actions.
